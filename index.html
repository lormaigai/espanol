High-value revision list

  • Indefinido for completed past events; imperfecto for background, habits, descriptions, ongoing states.
  • Closure patterns: preocupado por, ayudar a, separar en, cada vez, al aire libre, de forma equilibrada.
  • Writing rubric rewards clear communication, varied verbs, connectors, prepositions, pronouns, time markers, and subordinate clauses.
  • Reading answers should be short, direct, and copied or paraphrased from the exact paragraph.
  • Formal letters need register: Estimado/a, me dirijo a usted, le propongo, atentamente.

Indefinido vs imperfecto

Decision rules

Indefinido

Completed event, sequence, sudden action, defined time: ayer, el primer dia, finalmente, una vez.

Imperfecto

Background, description, repeated habit, age/time/weather, ongoing emotion or state: antes, siempre, de pequeno, mientras.

Both

Imperfecto sets the scene; indefinido interrupts or advances the story.

Writing score checklist

  • Hit every content bullet clearly. Do not sacrifice communication for fancy grammar.
  • Use past tenses accurately: indefinido for events, imperfecto for background, pluscuamperfecto for the earlier past.
  • Add connectors: primero, luego, sin embargo, ademas, por eso, por ultimo.
  • Add one opinion and one reason: en mi opinion, creo que, ya que, porque.
  • Keep it under 150 words. Short correct sentences beat long broken ones.
